  • Mango

  • The Alphonso mango is a foreign fruit that came to India through Goa along with Alfonso De Albuquerque, the Portuguese explorer and military strategist who led many Portuguese invasions in the 1600s. From Goa, the Alphonso mango traveled to different places like Ratnagiri, other Konkan region and southern side of India as well.The Devgad Alphonso gained distinctiveness and favorable characteristics like the pleasant fruity smell, no fiber, extremely aromatic and sweet taste, becoming popular among buyers. The distinctiveness comes from the geographical conditions of Devgad as the same is not observed in other varieties grown elsewhere.

  • Cashew Nuts

  • Cashew fruit, or cashew apple, is a fleshy accessory fruit that develops beneath the cashew nut.It is an oval or pear-shaped structure about 5–11 cm (2–41⁄4 in) long and has a yellow, orange, or red colour. It is edible and has a sweet and sour taste. It is a good source of vitamins C and A, fibre, and potassium.It can be eaten fresh, cooked, or juiced. It is often used in desserts like puddings, pies, and cakes. Juice from this fruit can be used to make drinks, such as smoothies and cocktails.Beneath the surface, the yellow flesh is spongy, fibrous, juicy, and soft but additionally stringy.
